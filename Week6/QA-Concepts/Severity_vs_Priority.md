# Severity vs Priority

## Severity

Severity indicates how serious a defect is and how much it affects the application.

Examples:
- Application crash
- Data loss
- Login failure

Severity is decided by the **Testing Team**.

---

## Priority

Priority indicates how quickly a defect should be fixed.

Examples:
- Typo on Home Page
- Company logo missing
- Incorrect product name

Priority is decided by the **Business Team / Project Manager**.

---

## Difference

| Severity | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Impact of defect | Urgency to fix |
| Tester decides | Business decides |
| Technical | Business |

## Example

Login button not working:
- Severity: High
- Priority: High

Typing mistake in company name:
- Severity: Low
- Priority: High
